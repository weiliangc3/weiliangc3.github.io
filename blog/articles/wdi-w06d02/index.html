Planning project 2

Written by Wei

In the morning I had an idea of how to fix all the bitters and bourbon etc problems I had. Then I had a look at the original fixes etc, and I realised why I was doing it all wrong, why I was struggling, and what was the best way of avoiding this problem forever. So I implemented all of it in the morning while we were going through migrations. I really shouldn’t do it, but I was quite excited about the prospect of never fighting the thing I’d been fighting for forever. And it worked, and now I think I’m fairly happy with the solution. It’s a small victory, for sure, but it made me quite happy. Two real take aways from this-

“rails g model Order customer:references etc:string” is a great way of linking things that I really should have realised was a thing. I’d thought about it over the weekend, and realised it was a rails only command that we hadn’t done yet.

Then we did validations, basically using validates: to make sure fields are filled in, and some stuff like object.errors and object.valid? to show what’s going on behind the hood in Rails.

The major part of the day we basically built a rails “Fiver” app, step by step, following a logical sequence.

Then we went drinking to plan our projects. Weird day.
