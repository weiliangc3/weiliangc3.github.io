Ruby Animal shelter homework

Written by Wei

Today we had outcomes, did a huge homework review of how to make blackjack, and learned how classes in ruby work and how inheritance works. Outcomes was interesting, got an old hand who came in and talked about his journey. I guess it’s probably a good idea for me to go out and network. I always feel a little eh about networking, I’m not naturally a very outgoing person, but I’ve sort of pushed myself towards that direction. Other than that I guess there’s not that much new news.

Classes in ruby… I understand them but I’m not sure if “object oriented programming” is something that you do in ruby. Feels a little weird? Like there aren’t really objects to orient to? I mean there are definitely objects to orient myself to but I don’t know if you straight up just program within an object like you do in Javascript

Homework was making a command-line ruby program simulating an animal shelter, with various functions like registering animals, registering people, etc etc. Conceptually not difficult, but I made it really hard by the amount of input checking I did and the fact that I fundamentally misunderstood the role of the animal shelter. Only 4 functions were requested but I ended up with 6. Oh well.

2 interesting bugs- one with require_relative and another with trying to use attr_accessor to redefine local variables within an instance of a class. I’ll try to ask tomorrow, I can’t really find an answer online.
