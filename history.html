Intergen

Development Intern at Intergen (From Generation to Generation)

Jan 2015 - May 2015

Before seriously embarking on learning how to code, I worked at a charity linking the elderly with schools to help provide support to schools while alleviating social isolation in the elderly. There, I generated statistics and analysis for marketing and management purposes as well as identifying resources and appropriate schools for expansion and development.

As part of a small 4 man adminstration team, I performed a variety of general administrative tasks such as advertising work opportunities, documenting business practices, filing, and technical support. Intergen has since been subsumed under its parent charity- From Generation to Generation.

Kings College London

BSc (Hons) in Medical Science from Kings College London

2008 - 2013

I did most of an MBBS Medicine programme before leaving in the final year. I competed all core and specialty medical rotations of the programme.

Manpower Officer for the Singapore Armed Forces

May 2007 - Feb 2008

As part of living in Singapore for more than 5 years while under 18, I got drafted into the Singapore Army. There, I went through Officer Training School to learn how to command a platoon of troops and did things like climb hills and survive in a Thai jungle.

After that I got posted to a manpower officer position at Basic Military Training Centre, where I did things like helping to organise events like graduation and intake parades, assigning training staff allocation, and doing other administrative things like being secretary to commander meetings

I served the Singapore army as a statutory requirement of living in Singapore. Read more about National Service here: Wikipedia link

Me in a bus with my best mates

Life Before Work

1987 - 2005

Before all that more important stuff, I did a whole lot of growing up kind of stuff. I started doing the growing thing after being born in Croydon, London. I think the first memories I actually have are of looking at ducks at a castle (Dover?).

I went to school for a little bit in England, but then I moved to Singapore, where I started school proper, and ended up being identified for Singapore's Gifted Education Programme. There, I met a whole bunch of really interesting people, and when I was in Secondary School, I started to play field hockey, swim, debate, write poety and participate in creative writing events, do science fairs, and learn karate. I also started taking photos for fun, trying to learn how to draw, learned some basic HTML and CSS, and sat around on ICQ and various MUDs.

Before working, I went to high school in Anglo-Chinese Junior College, where I played hockey and was part of the creative writing club. And (like virtually everyone else in my generation in South East Asia) started playing DOTA and reached my maximum height.
